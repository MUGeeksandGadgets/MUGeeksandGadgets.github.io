The Way of the Geek

Values

Geeks and Gadgets has three club values:

  • Make it fun!
  • Make it cool!
  • Make it open source!
We hold these values dear as they are the very core of what the club is about; having fun while make a cool open source project. We also highly value democracy and cooperation, two things that are absolutely neccesary for any project to come to a successful end. The geeks are all about being open and transparent, one of the reasons why we support open source. Don't believe us? Fine, we encourage the question everything attitude! Here is our Constitution so you can check it our for yourself!

Privacy is also a very high priority to the geeks. With social media integrating so heavily into our society and having the ability to google ourselves, does privacy still exist? We aim to educate people about what their rights are with technology and how to protect their privacy as well as how to better use that technology to get more out of life.

What is Open Source and Why do I Care?

Imagine source code as a type of recipe that your grandmother uses. It has been passed down and changed by each person who has used it. She gave it to your grandmother who changed it, then to your mother who again changed it and then shared it with her friends who changed it again. That is open souce; the idea that you should be free to give out your source code to others who can change it and share it again, making it better with each new set of eyes that see the code.

How is this beneficial? Simple. No one person knows everything, by allowing people to see and share the code, more people can contribute to it and make a better end product. What if it is a group of people working on it and not publishing code? The same principle applies, no single secluded group of people will have more expertise than the combined world.

This is not specific only to programmers. Engineers, Journalists, this idea applies to everyone in every discipline. It is the idea that information should be openly shared in hopes of learning from others and teaching them with an end goal of making the world a better place through learning. Granted, some things shouldn't be shared, but for the most part information should be. There are plenty of companies that share this belief and are built on it. Just to name a few: Mozilla, Red Hat, Facebook, and The Linux Foundation.

How does this idea affect you today? Perhaps you have heard of something called the internet. The internet was created with open source ideas in mind, and has thrived because of it. Open source gave birth to Linux, an open source operating system. Due to its open nature, Linux has thrived and you use it everyday. The stock market, smart TVs, and even nuclear submarines are powered by Linux. Pretty cool huh? Open source technology surrounds you and affects you everyday. Just imagine if more people would release those recipes, who knows what kind of pies we could make!

Officers

President

Patrick Shinn

Patrick is a freshman at Marshall majoring in Computer Science. He graduated number four in his class and with highest honors from Hebert Hoover High School where he served as year book photographer and as unofficial student IT support. Patrick is one of the original eight founding members and the club's first president. He aspires to someday build his own company to compete with Apple and Microsoft boasting a Linux driven operating system on both a desktop and mobile platform. Patrick is a strong supporter of open source as well as general information sharing. Special skill sets that Patrick possess are Linux installation and use, Linux server system administration, office software proficiency, graphic design, and general carpentry. Hobbies include photography, server maintenance, piloting remote control aircraft, pottery, general project construction, and spending time with friends.
